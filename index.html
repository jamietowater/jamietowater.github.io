About

Hands-on first.
Tech always.

I came up through Home repair, Property Maintenance and Operations — work that taught me how to diagnose problems fast, improvise solutions under pressure, and actually finish things. Not theory. Real stuff, with real consequences.

Over time, my interests shifted. Every time something broke I wanted to understand it deeper — not just fix it but know why it failed. That curiosity led me into electronics, firmware, networking, and eventually building tools from scratch.

Now I'm doing work I genuinely care about: embedded systems, FPV aircraft, 3D printing, self-hosted infrastructure, and custom electronics. Not because it looks good on a list, but because I can't stop building things.

I'm actively transitioning into tech — pursuing hardware-adjacent IT and support roles where practical problem-solving matters as much as the certificate.
